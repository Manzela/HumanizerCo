# HumanizerCo — Assignment #3: three ad creative variations before scaling spend

*Daniel Manzela · Assignment #3, reasoning write-up*

## 320 × 50 — Banner
This placement lives inside content feeds and app chrome where users are actively scrolling past everything. The audience is cold and distracted. The only job is pattern interruption plus one credible claim, so the entire creative is built around a single before/after proof point: "98% → 9% AI." That proof point makes the tool concrete and it signals the tool actually works, which addresses the primary skepticism in this category. "100% human" was deliberately removed, it triggers ad policy flags on Meta and TikTok, and it contradicts our own on-page disclaimers, which would break trust the moment someone clicks through. The 1px keyline creates visual continuity with the landing page, reducing the perceived gap between ad and destination. Hypothesis: this outperforms a brand/logo-led banner because proof converts cold traffic better than awareness in a category where trust is the barrier. (*especially given the fact that HumanizerCo has no existing brand presence, no audience, and no product equity.*)


## 250 × 250 — Square
Square placements get slightly more dwell time than banners and attract an audience that is one step warmer, they've seen the category before and are actively comparing options. The biggest barrier at this stage is not awareness, it's skepticism about whether the tool is legitimate. The transparency footer: "In-browser estimate · verify in any detector" is the core conversion mechanic here. It pre-empts the distrust before the click, so the user arrives at the landing page already partially sold on honesty rather than needing to be convinced of it there. The premium white card aesthetic mirrors the landing page directly, which shortens the cognitive jump between ad and product. Hypothesis: the transparency line lifts CTR versus a version without it, specifically among users who have been burned by overpromising tools before.


## 1080 × 1920 — Full‑screen mobile
This is the flagship format, it owns the entire screen for 3–5 seconds and has to land the full story in a single glance. The audience is mobile-first and arrives mid-session in a social or short-video context, so the emotional register needs to match: immediate, visual, no friction. The proof graphic is scaled up with a "Human ≤ 25%" marker because a concrete number reads faster than a headline at full-bleed scale. Ignoring platform safe zones is the most common reason full-screen creatives underperform despite strong copy, so pulling all key elements away from top and bottom UI overlays is non-negotiable here. The white card against dark app chrome creates the same landing page continuity as the square, but at a scale where it also functions as a thumb-stopper. Hypothesis: this format drives the highest CTR of the three due to format dominance, but may attract lower-intent clicks, worth tracking post-click engagement separately from the banner and square.
